There comes a last time for everything. For most things, we're not going to know.

The other day, my wife had the sudden realisation that our son, aged 15 months, was not crawling anymore. He was done. Probably never again were we going to see him on all fours, moving his butt in a cute swaying motion as he rushed towards his favourite ball in as frantic a manner as he could manage.

Just like that, he was done. And we didn't even know.

I imagine a movie where we go back into flashback mode to that last moment when he crawled. The camera zooms out and the viewer sees the baby and sees me sitting there. Maybe on my phone, maybe just lost in thought, worrying about that email I haven't responded to. [Narrator voice: _Little did he know that in front of his face, the baby was crawling for the last time_]

This is most obvious to me now with respect to my growing child. The change is rapid. He is picking up new skills every day. I can look back at all the things we used to do from the time he was born a little over a year ago. He doesn't lie on his little mat, crinkling his tiny eyes at the little blue bear hanging above anymore. He doesn't breastfeed anymore. He doesn't fit just on my forearms anymore.

And I'm now more aware of all the things that might end any day soon. One of the best parts of my day is when he falls asleep on my shoulder in the evening, in the dim red light of his bedroom. I hold onto him for just a few seconds more before I lay him down because feeling his slow breath on my chest is wonderful. But that little ritual is going to end. And so will so many other things that are fun and some that are annoying right now. Even the latter I know I will miss when it ends.

But I realised that it is not just with my baby. Everything I do in my life, with anyone, could be the last time. There are many things in my life that I have probably done for the last time. Some that I know of. I probably visited Panama for the last time in my life. That is to be expected.

There is probably someone who I have talked to for the last time in my life. There is probably someone I've seen for the last time in my life. The crucial part is that I don't know it or I've assumed it's not. I think I will talk to that person again or I will see that person again.

And If I knew that it was the last interaction I was going to have, I would have done it differently.

And so it goes with everyone. This might just be it. Last call.
